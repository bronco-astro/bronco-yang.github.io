Shiqi “Bronco” Yang

PhD Student in Physics · Rice University

I work at the intersection of observational astronomy, numerical modeling, and statistical analysis. My research interests focus on exoplanet detection and characterization, protoplanetary disks, and the physical processes behind planet formation.

I enjoy problems where careful data analysis and physical intuition meet, especially when observations and simulations inform each other.

Research Interests

  • Protoplanetary disk structure and evolution
  • Planet formation and disk–planet interactions
  • Astronomical instrumentation and adaptive optics
  • Statistical methods for astronomical data

Research Experience

My research experience spans both observational data analysis and numerical simulation.

I have worked on simulations of protoplanetary disks using Athena++, with an emphasis on disk dynamics and interpretation of simulation results. I have also analyzed JWST MIRI observations, including continuum subtraction and molecular line analysis.

In addition, I have experience working with astronomical survey data, including light curve analysis and statistical characterization.
